## Title of the category

This category of Risks describes various factors related to urban areas and population density that contribute to the spread of contagious diseases. It includes issues such as spatial inequalities in mobility patterns, population mobility, urban density and crowding, overcrowding in urban areas, urban activities and points of interests, safety concerns in public transport, heterogeneity of state policies, overcrowding in public places, disrupted travel and tourism industry, population growth and urbanization, impact on transportation infrastructure, inadequate public transportation infrastructure, difficulties in ensuring social distancing, crowded living conditions, increased contact in workplaces and crowded shops, loss of urban green spaces, variation in car parking standards, infrastructure challenges, streets being re-designed, and overcrowding during events or in specific locations. Overall, these risks highlight the vulnerability of densely populated areas and the challenges faced in containing the spread of diseases in such environments.
