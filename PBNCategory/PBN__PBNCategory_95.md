## Title of the category

This category of Risks describes various modes and routes of contagious diseases spread in infrastructure, such as green buildings neighbourhoods. It includes transmission through disease carriers, surfaces, close contact, direct and indirect contact, air, droplets, respiratory mechanisms, and travel-related activities. It also considers specific types of infectious diseases, such as influenza, tuberculosis, malaria, and zoonotic diseases, as well as the impact of interventions on transmission rates. Other factors to consider are asymptomatic transmission, fecal-oral transmission, hidden transmission, and transmission between individuals within a community or from mother to child. This list also addresses the potential for bacterial growth and transmission and the role of different mediums in facilitating the spread of diseases.
