## Title of the category

This category of Risks describes various issues related to indoor and outdoor air quality in the built environment. It includes factors such as allergic respiratory responses to pollen, indoor air contamination and pollution, inadequate ventilation and air filtration, poor air circulation, accumulation of dirt and microbial growth in HVAC systems, and the spread of diseases through airborne transmission. These Risks also highlight the potential health hazards associated with pollutants like volatile organic compounds (VOCs), ozone emissions, and secondhand smoke exposure. Overall, this list encompasses the wide range of concerns and challenges surrounding air quality in both residential and commercial buildings, and the individuals mentioned may be affected by these issues or contribute to them in some way.
