# __Assumption of homogeneous mixing in metropolitan areas can bias model results and lead to inaccurate epidemic size predictions.__

## Articles mentionning the risk

* [Brizuela_understanding_2019](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Article/PBN__Article_421.md)

## Mitigations of this risk

* [Utilize spatially-explicit disease transmission models to account for the spatial organization of human mobility, quantify the impact of population size and density on disease transmission, and adjust the model assumptions accordingly.](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Mitigation/PBN__Mitigation_7815.md)

## Name of the risk

Inaccurate predictions of epidemic size can result in inadequate resource allocation and preparedness for infectious outbreaks.
