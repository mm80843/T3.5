# __Covariate measurements such as living alone, education, and occupational social class may not fully capture the complex socioeconomic and environmental factors influencing health outcomes, leading to confounding effects.__

## Articles mentionning the risk

* [Corley_home_2021](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Article/PBN__Article_445.md)

## Mitigations of this risk

* [Collect additional contextual data on environmental variables, neighborhood characteristics, and broader socioeconomic factors to account for potential confounders in the analysis.](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Mitigation/PBN__Mitigation_9291.md)

## Name of the risk

Potential confounding of study results due to inadequate consideration of all relevant socioeconomic and environmental variables.
