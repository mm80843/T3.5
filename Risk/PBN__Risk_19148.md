# __Dependency of other tick-borne diseases on the presence of deer, leading to increased risk of transmission and spread.__

## Articles mentionning the risk

* [Lohmus_making_2015](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Article/PBN__Article_665.md)

## Mitigations of this risk

* [Implementing strategies to manage the population and distribution of cervides, as well as developing targeted interventions for controlling diseases dependent on deer.](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Mitigation/PBN__Mitigation_21120.md)

## Name of the risk

Elevated likelihood of transmission and spread of tick-borne diseases such as Babesia spp. and Anaplasma due to the presence of cervides.
