# __Insufficient consideration of qualitative aspects in quantitative resilience approaches may lead to overlooking the socio-behavioral factors that influence contagious disease spread.__

## Articles mentionning the risk

* [Hosseini_review_2016](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Article/PBN__Article_487.md)

## Mitigations of this risk

* [Integrate qualitative data on human behavior and social factors into quantitative models of contagious disease spread, and consider behavioral patterns in resilience assessment.](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Mitigation/PBN__Mitigation_11495.md)

## Name of the risk

Failure to account for human behavior and social dynamics could result in ineffective strategies for controlling disease transmission, leading to increased risk of outbreaks.
