# __Dependence on manual control for lighting and dynamic envelope elements may result in suboptimal use of natural light and ventilation, leading to indoor air quality issues and increased risk of airborne disease transmission.__

## Articles mentionning the risk

* [Pedersen_assessment_2022](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Article/PBN__Article_614.md)

## Mitigations of this risk

* [Implement automated lighting and dynamic envelope control systems to optimize natural light usage and ventilation, improving indoor air quality and reducing the risk of airborne disease transmission.](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Mitigation/PBN__Mitigation_18682.md)

## Name of the risk

Potential for poor indoor air quality, uneven temperature distribution, and insufficient natural light, increasing the risk of airborne disease transmission.
