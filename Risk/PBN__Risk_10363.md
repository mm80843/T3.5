# __The emotional need for ’nature’ is explained as an inherited affection from the experience of choosing habitats and building dwellings, which can lead to a preference for natural characteristics in modern architectural spaces.__

## Articles mentionning the risk

* [Zhong_biophilic_2022](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Article/PBN__Article_498.md)

## Mitigations of this risk

* [Architects and urban planners can incorporate natural characteristics into modern architectural spaces to address the emotional need for ’nature’ and promote well-being and sense of place.](https://github.com/mm80843/T3.5/blob/pages/Mitigation/PBN__Mitigation_12271.md)

## Name of the risk

If this preference for natural characteristics is not considered in architectural design, it may result in a lack of connection to the built environment and potentially impact well-being and sense of place.
